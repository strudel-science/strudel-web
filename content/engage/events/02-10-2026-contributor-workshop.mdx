The STRUDEL team is excited to host an <b>in-person</b> contribution event at the AI Futures Lab on Tuesday February 10th, 2026. This event will foster a wide range of contributions to the STRUDEL open source project that is creating and stewarding resources to enable more usable scientific software experiences. Experience with the STRUDEL resources is NOT required! We anticipate sessions between 9am-4:30pm with a networking hour afterwards. Dropping in & out as your schedule allows is completely fine. <b>Light breakfast, lunch, and snacks will be provided along with opportunities to network with a range of community members.</b>

### Who should join?
Join us if you are interested in:
- Contributing to open source science user experience (UX) / user interface (UI) design resources - ideas, design, code, documentation, website, etc.
- Trying out different AI tools & models in the design and development of open source resources
- Being recognized as a contributor to the STRUDEL project with a contributor certificate & recognition on the GitHub/website
- Networking with expert practitioners from a range of scientific software and open source communities

### What will we do?
Our contribution pathways support a wide range of skills and activities. Participants will be able to select the pathway that suits them best:
- Design: Collaboratively generate concepts, ideas, and visuals to expand the STRUDEL Design System
- Code: Build new features for our software toolkit in TypeScript or Python.
- Websites: Write and review content on our public website and docs site and ideate on how information is presented.
- Curriculum: Improve our learning resources with new content, lessons, or tutorials.

## The STRUDEL Open Source Effort

STRUDEL is an open source community building resources to enable more usable scientific software. Our mission is advocating for and advancing the inclusion of user experience (UX) practices & tools in scientific software development.

STRUDEL stewards multiple key products. Our scientific Design System provides adaptable user interface patterns with <a href="https://strudel.science/design-system/task-flows/overview/" target="_blank">interface templates</a>, <a href="https://github.com/strudel-science/strudel-kit" target="_blank">reusable code</a>, and <a href="https://strudel.science/strudel-kit/docs/" target="_blank">documentation</a>. The open <a href="https://carpentries-incubator.github.io/rapid-usability-tutorial/" target="_blank">curriculum</a>   develops educational resources to help a wide range of stakeholders learn how to conduct user experience activities as part of their scientific software development. You can learn more on our website https://strudel.science/ 

Have ideas for additional products that advance this mission? Let us know and plan to join us!
